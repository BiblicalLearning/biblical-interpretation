A Framework for Biblical Interpretation

Rightly Dividing the Word of Truth — 2 Timothy 2:15

Foundation: Biblical understanding is a spiritual gift. The Spirit teaches us; our methods serve us. Begin with prayer, not analysis.

The Foundation: Spiritual Illumination

  • Natural man cannot receive spiritual things (1 Cor. 2:14)
  • Spirit of truth guides into all truth (John 16:13)
  • Carnal mind cannot perceive spiritual truth (Rom. 8:6-7)
  • Come with humility, expect transformation
1 Cor. 2:14 — "They are spiritually discerned"

Question 1: When Was This Written?

Why: Historical timeframe affects urgency, warnings, fulfillment

Ask: Date? Roman Empire events? Temple standing?

Example: Thessalonians written AD 50-51, before Jerusalem's fall

Question 2: Who Was the Original Audience?

Why: Written FOR us but not TO us

Ask: Jewish/Gentile? Struggles? Relationship with author?

Example: Hebrews = Jewish Christians tempted to return to Temple

Question 3: What Is the Central Topic?

Why: Prevents proof-texting and distorted theology

Ask: Problem addressed? Flow of thought? Repeated words?

Tip: Read entire chapter/letter before examining details

Question 4: Who Is Speaking?

Why: Authority depends on the speaker

Ask: Author's teaching? Jesus quoted? OT reference? Opponent's view being refuted?

Example: "All things lawful" = Corinthian slogan, not Paul's teaching

Key: Descriptive ≠ Prescriptive

Question 5: OT Fulfillment?

Why: NT saturated with OT connections

Ask: Direct quotes? Original context? How applied?

Key: When NT says "fulfilled," take it seriously

Example: Matthew's "that it might be fulfilled" statements

Question 6: Future Fulfillment?

Why: Distinguish fulfilled from still-future

Ask: Time indicators? ("soon," "at hand," "this generation")

Ask: Who is "you"? Has it occurred in history?

Example: Rev. 1:1, 3 — "must soon take place," "time is near"

Study Method:

  1. Begin with prayer
  2. Establish timeframe
  3. Identify audience
  4. Find main point
  5. Determine speaker
  6. Trace OT connections
  7. Assess future references carefully
  8. End with reflection
Goal: Move from "What does this mean to me?" to "What did this mean to them, and how does that truth apply to my life?"
